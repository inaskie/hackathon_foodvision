Project Background

Born from a 24-hour Hackathon at the E³UDRES² European University Alliance

Project Origin

FoodVision was born during an intensive 24-hour Hackathon organized by the E³UDRES² European University Alliance. The Hackathon took place at the Fulda Hub, focusing on the Rhön region in Germany, an area with a history of division.

The core of our team consisted of seven interdisciplinary students who came together to tackle a critical challenge: How can we use cuisine to shape regional identities and connect Europe at the same time?

An International, Interdisciplinary Vision

The team represented the cultural breadth of Europe, with students hailing from Germany, Belgium, Portugal, Poland, and Latvia. Despite their diverse academic backgrounds, the shared conviction was clear: cuisine functions as a universal language capable of transcending historical and geographical boundaries.

Our Mission

To bring people from all over Europe together through food. By creating FoodVision, we offer a platform to promote local farmers and artisanal specialties and connect them with other countries and cultures.

Our Core Philosophy

We demonstrate that regional pride and European cooperation are complementary goals. FoodVision creates a space where local traditions are celebrated while simultaneously building bridges between cultures.

What started as a quick conceptual solution has evolved into a movement to celebrate the gastronomic diversity of Europe, support local producers, and promote sustainable food cultures.

Through FoodVision, we address a fundamental question: How can regions maintain their unique culinary identities while actively participating in broader European cultural exchange?

The Fulda Hub & Rhön Region

The project emerged from the unique context of the Rhön region in Germany, an area with a history of division. This geographical and historical setting provided valuable insight into how food and regional identity can serve as tools for connection and reconciliation.

The Fulda Hub, as part of the E³UDRES² European University Alliance, provided the collaborative environment necessary for developing this interdisciplinary, pan-European concept. The 24-hour format challenged the team to rapidly synthesize diverse perspectives into a coherent, actionable vision.

From Concept to Reality

FoodVision represents more than a competition format—it is a framework for celebrating Europe's culinary heritage while building meaningful connections between regions, producers, and people.

The journey from a 24-hour Hackathon concept to a comprehensive platform continues, driven by the belief that food can unite while preserving what makes each region unique.
